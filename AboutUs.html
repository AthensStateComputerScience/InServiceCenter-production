In Service Center

The Athens State University Regional In-Service Center’s (ASRIC) purpose is to provide professional development activities for the approximately 4,500 K-12 educators in ten school systems: Athens City, Blount County, Cullman City, Cullman County, Decatur City, Hartselle City, Lawrence County, Limestone County, Morgan County, and Oneonta City. The ASRIC will develop workshops to meet the identified needs of a specific school or school system. We sponsor book studies regionally and within LEAs, host face-to-face, virtual and blended professional development in school, system or regionally based formats. The ASRIC was established by the Alabama legislature in 1984-85 as one of eleven regional in-service centers in the state.

Building/Reservation Information

The Athens State AMSTI/In-Service Center is comprised of offices for the In-Service Center, AMSTI, ASIM, and TIM; AMSTI warehouse; and 3 training rooms. These rooms are available for our systems and the ALSDE to host LEA professional development and meetings. Our three meeting rooms are equipped with interactive boards, projectors, document cameras, and tables and chairs. The room capacities are all approximate, and are as follows. Room A, located at the front of the building accommodates up to 28 people at tables and chairs. Room B, in the rear of the building, will accommodate up to 32 people at tables and chairs. Room C, in the rear of the building, will accommodate up to 60 at tables and chairs. Please click here to view the calendar for available dates, and complete the form for room reservation requests.

ASRIC Governing Board Information

The Athens State Regional In-Service Center’s governing board is comprised of eight classroom teachers, four administrators, two higher education representatives, one school board member from one of our 10 systems, and an Alabama State Department Representative. Interested in serving the board and assisting with selecting professional development policies, and helping choose the path our Regional In-Service Center is taking? Contact us! We are filling the following vacancies on the board before the beginning of the 2017-2018 school year: two teachers, large system; and an administrator and teacher, small system.

Alabama Math, Science, and Technology Initiative

The Alabama Math, Science, and Technology Initiative (AMSTI) is the Alabama Department of Education’s initiative to improve math and science teaching statewide. AMSTI-Athens was established in 2008. Its mission is to provide all students in Grades K-12 with the knowledge and skills needed for success in the workforce and/or postsecondary studies. The premise behind AMSTI is that students learn math and science best by doing math and science, especially when they are able to relate it to their daily lives. Schools apply to become official AMSTI Schools and, when accepted, agree to send all of their math and science teachers and administrators to two-week long Summer Institutes for two consecutive years. At the elementary level, where most teachers are self-contained (teaching all subjects), entire faculties attend the institutes. Each AMSTI Site also runs a large Materials Refurbishment Center where all of the kits from the region are refurbished to “like new” condition before being sent to the next teacher. AMSTI Sites resemble large warehouses where used kits are refurbished to like new condition before being sent back out to teachers. More information can be found at www.amsti.org.
